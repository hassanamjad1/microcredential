NFT'S EXPLAINED

NFTs can really be anything digital (such as drawings, music, your brain downloaded and turned into an AI), but a lot of the current excitement is around using the tech to sell digital art.

What is NFT?

NFTs have been used as a speculative asset, and they have drawn increasing criticism for the energy cost and carbon footprint associated with validating blockchain transactions as well as their frequent use in art scams and claimed structure of the NFT market to be a ponzi scheme. NFTs are the digital equivalent to Trading Cards since they are both unique and decentralized, for example physically giving cards away and basing conditions on how old the nft/trading card is. NFTs can been used to sell digital downloads of a product. Where there can be an infinite amount of say digital files which can be copied easily or cloned perfectly, Nfts cannot be duplicated so easily, not according the blockchain, as no two nfts can have the same ID. The original digital download becomes an asset according to the nft baked into the file and can therefore be sold or traded, as another copy when checked against the main ledger or blockchain will be denied permission to run as those conditions and features will not line up with the original.
